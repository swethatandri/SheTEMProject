Career Paths

Science

Astronomer

An astronomer is a scientist in the field of astronomy who focuses their studies on a specific question or field outside the scope of Earth. They observe astronomical objects such as stars, planets, moons, comets and galaxies in either observational or theoretical astronomy.


Chemist

Chemists study how chemicals affect each other, and how they interact with the environment. They conduct experiments in laboratories, and analyze results and data. Most chemists use databases, scientific software, graphics, and design and photo imaging tools in their work.


Doctor/Surgeon

Doctors/Surgeon, also known as Physicians, are licensed health professionals who maintain and restore human health through the practice of medicine. They examine patients, review their medical history, diagnose illnesses or injuries, administer treatment, and counsel patients on their health and well being.


Physicist

Physicists often conduct, investigate, and conclude solutions or theories in science. They also design scientific equipment for example electron microscopes, particle accelerators, and lasers that can be used to perform experiments.


Researcher

A researcher is responsible for designing, undertaking, and analysing information from controlled laboratory-based investigations, experiments, and trials in hopes of making a scientific conclusion.


Technology

AI Researcher

One role for an AI specialist is to program computers to test hypotheses in relation to how the human mind works, through cognitive simulation. The majority of AI specialists work in applied AI, their purpose is to program computer smart systems.


Back-End Developer

A back-end developer is someone who builds and maintains the technology needed to power the components which enable the user-facing side of the website to exist. This includes the core application logic, databases, data and application integration, API and other back end processes.


Cyber Security Analyst

Cyber Security jobs inform system assets by identifying and solving potential and actual security problems. Protects the system by defining access privileges, control structures, and resources. Recognizes problems by identifying abnormalities; reporting violations.


Front-End Developer

Front end development manages everything that users visually see first in their browser or application. Front end developers will be engaged in analyzing code, design, and debugging applications along with ensuring a seamless user experience.



Graphic Designer

The Graphic Designer job description includes the entire process of defining requirements, visualizing and creating graphics including illustrations, logos, layouts and photos. You'll be the one to shape the visual aspects of websites, books, magazines, product packaging, exhibitions and more.


Software Engineer

A software engineer uses engineering and programming languages to create new technological products, computer games, and manage control systems.


VR Developer

Someone who works in virtual reality creates simulations of real world experiences. Some examples of VR include virtual rides, virtual games, and even career/job simulators

Engineering

Aerospace Engineer

Aerospace engineers work in industries that design or manufacture aircraft, missiles, national defense systems, or spacecraft. They mainly work for production companies, analysis and design firms, research and development firms, and the federal government.


Biomedical Engineer

Biomedical engineers focus on advances in technology and medicine to develop new devices and equipment for improving human health. For example, they might design software to run medical equipment or computer simulations to test new drug therapies.


Chemical Engineer

Chemical engineering is the branch of engineering that deals with chemical production and the manufacture of products through chemical processes. This includes designing equipment, systems and processes for refining raw materials and for mixing, compounding and processing chemicals to make valuable products.


Civil Engineer

Civil engineers design major transportation projects. Civil engineers conceive, design, build, supervise, operate, construct and maintain infrastructure projects and systems in the public and private sector, including roads, buildings, airports, tunnels, dams, bridges, and systems for water supply and sewage treatment.


Computer Engineer

Computer hardware engineers research, design, develop, and test computer systems and components such as circuit boards. Computer hardware engineers research, design, develop, and test computer systems and components such as processors, circuit boards, memory devices, networks, and routers.


Data Engineer

Data engineering is the aspect of data science that focuses on practical applications of data collection and analysis. For all the work that data scientists do to answer questions using large sets of information, there have to be mechanisms for collecting and validating that information.


Electric Engineer

Electrical engineers apply the principles of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism to develop electrical products and systems. They perform risk assessments and ensure compliance with safety standards and electrical engineering codes. They also conduct research to create new applications.


Material Engineer

Materials engineers work with metals, ceramics, and plastics to create new materials. Materials engineers develop, process, and test materials used to create a range of products, from computer chips and aircraft wings to golf clubs and biomedical devices.


Mechanical Engineer

A Mechanical engineer designs power-producing machines, such as electric generators, engines, turbines, refrigerators, air condition systems. Mechanical engineers design other machines inside buildings like elevators as well.

Math

Economist

Economists study the production and distribution of resources, goods, and services by collecting and analyzing data, researching trends, and evaluating economic issues.


Financial Analyst

A financial analyst is a professional, undertaking financial analysis for external or internal clients as a core feature of the job. The role may specifically be titled securities analyst, research analyst, equity analyst, investment analyst, or ratings analyst.


Forensic Analysis

Forensic scientists are who help to collect, preserve, and examine physical evidence during the course of an investigation. Forensic Scientist must be methodical, accurate and unbiased.


Math Professor

Provides tutoring and academic counseling to students, maintains classes related records, and assesses student coursework. Collaborates and supports colleagues regarding research interests and co-curricular activities. They teach a variety of courses in the math subject.


Statistician

A statistician is someone who specializes in statistics, the process of analyzing numerical data. They design surveys, questionnaires, experiments, and polls to collect data and make sense of it.


Cartographer

Cartographers are concerned with all aspects of map-making (scientific, technological and artistic). They are responsible for: researching, collecting, storing, retrieving, evaluating and manipulating data. designing maps.


Cryptanalyst

Cryptanalysts secure computer and information technology systems by creating algorithms and ciphers to encrypt data. They often also carry out the duties of a cryptanalyst, deciphering algorithms and cipher text to decrypt information.
